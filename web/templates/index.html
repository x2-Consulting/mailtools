    DNS Checks
    MX Records —

    What: MX records tell the internet which servers accept email for your domain.

    Why: Without correct MX records, nobody can send you email. Priority values ensure failover to backup servers.

    SPF —

    What: SPF lists which servers are authorised to send email from your domain.

    Why: Prevents spammers forging your domain. A strict -all policy means unauthorised senders are rejected.

    Record Generator
    SPF Include Chain —

    What: Recursively expands all include: directives to show every authorised IP address.

    Why: SPF has a hard limit of 10 DNS lookups. Exceeding it causes a permerror and email may be rejected.

    DMARC —

    What: DMARC ties SPF and DKIM together and tells receivers what to do when email fails authentication.

    Why: Without DMARC reject, anyone can send email appearing to be from your domain.

    Record Generator
    DKIM —

    What: DKIM adds a cryptographic signature to outgoing email so recipients can verify it hasn't been altered.

    Why: Required for DMARC alignment. Use 2048-bit RSA keys — 1024-bit is weak and should be rotated.

    Connectivity
    SMTP Connection Tests —

    What: Tests live TCP connections to your mail servers on ports 25 (SMTP), 587 (Submission), and 465 (SMTPS).

    Why: STARTTLS on port 25/587 encrypts mail in transit. Servers without TLS expose message content to interception.

    Open Relay & Catch-All —

    Open Relay: Tests whether your server forwards email to arbitrary external addresses. An open relay is immediately exploited by spammers and will get your IP blacklisted.

    Catch-All: Tests whether the server accepts email to any address at your domain. Catch-alls cause high bounce rates that damage deliverability reputation.

    Reputation
    Blacklists (RBL) —

    What: Real-time Blackhole Lists (RBLs) are databases of IPs known to send spam. Checked: Spamhaus, Barracuda, SpamCop, TrendMicro ERS/DBL, and 19 others.

    Why: A listing causes email to be silently dropped or spam-foldered at major providers. Contact the RBL operator for delisting.

    Domain Info (WHOIS) —

    What: Registration data — age, registrar, expiry, and status flags.

    Why: Very new domains (<30 days) are penalised by spam filters. Expired domains stop delivering email. Status flags protect against domain hijacking.

    IP Reputation —

    What: Organisation, country, network block and abuse contact for each MX server IP via RDAP.

    Why: Confirms mail is sent from expected infrastructure. Abuse contact useful for reporting incidents.

    Advanced Optional
    BIMI —

    What: BIMI displays your brand logo next to authenticated emails in Gmail, Apple Mail, and Yahoo.

    Why: Requires DMARC quarantine or reject. A Verified Mark Certificate (VMC) is needed for Gmail. Improves brand trust and open rates.

    Record Generator
    MTA-STS & TLS-RPT —

    MTA-STS: Forces other mail servers to use TLS when sending to you, and reject connections with invalid certificates.

    TLS-RPT: Tells other servers where to send reports when TLS connections to your MX fail.

    Why: Without MTA-STS, email can be downgraded to plaintext via STARTTLS stripping, exposing message content.

    DNS Record Generator
    DANE / TLSA —

    What: DANE pins your mail server's TLS certificate in DNS via TLSA records, eliminating reliance on the CA trust system.

    Why: Prevents TLS certificate spoofing on MX servers. Required by some strict receivers (e.g. German mail providers). Requires DNSSEC.
