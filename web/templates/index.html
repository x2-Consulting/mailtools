    MX Records —

    What: MX (Mail Exchanger) records tell the internet which servers accept email for your domain.

    Why: Without correct MX records, nobody can send you email. Priority values ensure failover to backup servers.

    SPF —

    What: SPF (Sender Policy Framework) lists which servers are authorised to send email from your domain.

    Why: Prevents spammers forging your domain in the "From" address. A strict -all policy means unauthorised senders are rejected.

    Record Generator
    SPF Include Chain —

    What: Recursively expands all include: directives in your SPF record to show every authorised IP address.

    Why: SPF has a hard limit of 10 DNS lookups. Exceeding it causes a permerror and all your email may be rejected. This view shows the full tree and total lookup count.

    DMARC —

    What: DMARC ties SPF and DKIM together and tells receivers what to do when email fails authentication: none (monitor), quarantine (spam folder), or reject (block).

    Why: Without DMARC, anyone can send email appearing to be from your domain and it will be delivered. DMARC + reject is the only way to fully prevent domain spoofing.

    Record Generator
    DKIM —

    What: DKIM adds a cryptographic signature to outgoing emails so the recipient can verify the message hasn't been tampered with in transit.

    Why: Required for DMARC alignment. Without DKIM, forwarded mail often fails SPF, and DMARC can't protect you. Use 2048-bit RSA keys — 1024-bit is considered weak.

    BIMI —

    What: BIMI displays your brand logo next to authenticated emails in Gmail, Apple Mail, and Yahoo.

    Why: Requires DMARC policy of quarantine or reject. A Verified Mark Certificate (VMC) is required for Gmail logo display. Improves brand recognition and trust.

    Record Generator
    MTA-STS & TLS-RPT —

    MTA-STS: Forces other mail servers to use TLS when sending to you, and reject connections with invalid certificates. Requires a DNS record + policy file at https://mta-sts.yourdomain.com/.well-known/mta-sts.txt.

    TLS-RPT: Tells other servers where to send reports when TLS connections to your MX fail. Essential for monitoring MTA-STS effectiveness.

    Why: Without MTA-STS, email to your domain can be downgraded to plaintext (STARTTLS stripping attack) exposing message content.

    DNS Record Generator
    DANE / TLSA —

    What: DANE (DNS-based Authentication of Named Entities) pins your mail server's TLS certificate in DNS via TLSA records, eliminating reliance on the CA trust system.

    Why: Prevents TLS certificate spoofing on your MX servers. Required by some strict receivers (e.g. German mail providers). Requires DNSSEC on your domain.

    Blacklists (RBL) —

    What: Real-time Blackhole Lists (RBLs) are databases of IP addresses known to send spam. Checked: Spamhaus, Barracuda, SpamCop, TrendMicro ERS/DBL, and 19 others.

    Why: A listing can cause all your email to be silently dropped or spam-foldered at major providers. If listed, contact the RBL operator for delisting.

    IP Reputation —

    What: Shows the organisation, country, network block and abuse contact for each MX server IP via RDAP.

    Why: Confirms your mail is being sent from expected infrastructure. The abuse contact is useful if someone is forging your domain or if you need to report an incident.

    Domain Info (WHOIS) —

    What: Registration data for the domain — age, registrar, expiry, and status flags.

    Why: Very new domains (<30 days) are heavily penalised by spam filters. Expired domains stop delivering email. clientDeleteProhibited/clientTransferProhibited flags protect against domain hijacking.

    SMTP Connection Tests —

    What: Tests live TCP connections to your mail servers on ports 25 (SMTP), 587 (Submission), and 465 (SMTPS), verifying TLS support and certificate details.

    Why: STARTTLS on port 25/587 encrypts mail in transit. Port 465 uses implicit TLS. Servers without TLS expose email content to interception on every hop.

    Open Relay & Catch-All —

    Open Relay: Tests whether your mail server will forward email to arbitrary external addresses. An open relay is immediately exploited by spammers and will get your IP blacklisted within hours.

    Catch-All: Tests whether the server accepts email to any address at your domain (e.g. gibberish@yourdomain.com). Catch-alls cause high bounce rates that damage deliverability reputation.
